ARCSat

The Amateur Radio Communications Satellites (ARCSat) is a 3U nanosatellite for the purposes of amateur radio communications.

Vibration Testing

Led the vibration testing of ARCSat which is a critical verification step before launch. Organized testing requirements with NTS testing facilities, built the vibration assembly, and analyzed vibration results after the test.

Thermal Vacuum Chamber Testing

Organized and performed thermal vacuum chamber testing to ensure satellite performance can withstand the thermal and vacuum environment of orbit.

TVAC Test Plan

Ground Station

Building the ground station software infrastructure in GNURadio and Python to automatically transmit and receive scheduled satellite commands.

Ground Station Code Ground Station Overview

Hardstack Assembly

Aided in the full assembly of the nanosatellite in a clean room environment in order to survive vibration testing and launch.

Mechanical Design

Developed Mechanical Interfacing Control Documents for key PCBs. Modelled TVAC Interfacing Plate in CAD for machining and use during TVAC testing. Modelled Solar Panel covers in CAD for 3D printing and use during transport.
